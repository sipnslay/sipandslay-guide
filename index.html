Trending Music

How to find and use trending audio for maximum reach
Why Trending Audio Matters
Instagram pushes reels with trending audio

Using a trending song can 2-5x your reach compared to no audio or an unpopular track. Instagram's algorithm actively promotes reels that use audio it's currently pushing. This is the single easiest hack for free reach.

How to Find Trending Audio (3 Methods)
1The Arrow Method (Easiest) - Open Instagram > tap Reels tab (bottom center). Scroll through reels. When you see a song name at the bottom-left with a small upward arrow next to it, that audio is trending. Tap the song name > "Save audio" to save it for later.
2The Explore Method - Go to Explore (magnifying glass). Watch the reels that show up. Notice which songs keep repeating across different creators. If you hear the same audio 3+ times in 10 minutes of scrolling, it's trending. Tap the audio name > Save.
3The Audio Library - When creating a reel, tap "Audio" at the top. Instagram shows "Trending" and "For You" sections. Pick from there. Songs with a trending icon get priority from the algorithm.
How to Add Music to Your Reel (Step by Step)
1Edit your video first (add character overlay, captions, everything)
2In Instagram (or Meta Business Suite), start creating a reel and upload your video
3Tap "Audio" or the music note icon at the top
4Search for the trending song you saved, or browse "Trending"
5Select the song. Use the slider to pick which part of the song plays.
6Adjust the volume: turn the music DOWN (10-20%) so the podcast/clip audio stays clear. The music is background texture, not the main event.
7Preview it. If the music overpowers the speaking, turn it down more. If it's a reaction clip with no speaking, music can be louder (40-60%).
Tips for Picking the Right Audio
Match the mood

Motivational clip = uplifting/inspirational music. Funny clip = upbeat/quirky music. Emotional moment = soft/dramatic music. The music should amplify the feeling, not fight it.

Use instrumental or low-vocal tracks

For reaction/podcast clips where someone is talking, pick instrumentals or songs with minimal lyrics. Two voices at once (the clip + the song) is confusing. Save lyrical songs for text-overlay reels with no speaking.

Save 5-10 trending audios every week

Spend 10 minutes on Sunday scrolling reels and saving every trending audio you spot. Then when you're posting during the week, you have a ready library. Trending audio changes fast, so refresh weekly.

Always use Instagram's music library

Never import music from outside (like adding a song in CapCut). Instagram can detect it and won't promote the reel. Always add music through Instagram's built-in audio tool so it gets the algorithm boost.

Quick Save Habit
Every time you scroll Instagram, save audio

Hear a song you like on a reel? Tap the song name at the bottom > "Save audio". Build a collection. When it's time to post, you'll have trending options ready. Think of it like collecting ingredients before cooking.

Instagram Guide

Everything you need to know - especially coming from YouTube
One-Time Setup (Do This First)
1. Install Meta Business Suite (free app)

This is how you schedule posts. Instagram's own app can't schedule. Download "Meta Business Suite" from the App Store, log in with your Facebook account, and connect your Instagram account. If you don't have a Facebook Page, it'll ask you to create one - just make a simple one called "Sip & Slay", it won't be public.

2. Switch to Creator Account

Instagram > Settings > Account > Switch to Professional Account > Creator. This unlocks analytics (who's watching, when, where) and lets Meta Business Suite manage your posts. It's free, no downsides.

3. Install Canva (free app)

For making cover images (like YouTube thumbnails but vertical). Use the "Instagram Reel Cover" template - it's already the right size (1080x1920). Add text, your character, make it eye-catching.

YouTube vs Instagram - Key Differences
Cover Image = Thumbnail

Instagram calls it a "cover image". You set it during upload in Meta Business Suite (tap "Edit cover"). Unlike YouTube, it's vertical (9:16). Your profile grid shows a cropped square from the center, so keep the main text/face in the middle. Make these in Canva - takes 2 minutes.

Captions are SHORT

Not like YouTube descriptions. 2-3 punchy lines max. A hook line, a context line, and a call-to-action ("Follow for daily chai wisdom"). That's it. Check the Captions tab for ready-made ones.

Hashtags Go in First Comment

Not in the caption. After you post (or schedule), immediately add a comment with your hashtags. This keeps the caption clean and the hashtags still work. Use the Hashtags tab to pick and copy a set.

No Long Descriptions, No Tags, No Cards

Instagram has none of YouTube's extras. No end screens, no info cards, no long SEO descriptions. Your only tools are: the video itself, the caption, the cover image, hashtags, and the audio you pick. Keep it simple.

How to Schedule a Reel (Step by Step)
1Open Meta Business Suite app on your phone
2Tap the blue "Create" button at the bottom > "Reel"
3Select your edited video from camera roll
4Write your caption (2-3 lines - grab one from the Captions tab)
5Tap "Edit cover" > choose "Upload from gallery" > pick your Canva cover image
6Tap "Schedule for later" at the bottom (instead of "Share now")
7Pick the date and time (check Times tab for best posting windows)
8Tap "Schedule" - done! It'll post automatically at that time
9When it goes live, open Instagram and add your hashtags as the first comment
Algorithm Tips
1Post every day for 30 days. Consistency is how the algorithm learns to trust your account.
2Watch time is king. A 15s reel watched fully beats a 60s reel watched halfway.
3Saves > Shares > Comments > Likes. Optimize for saves by making content people want to revisit.
4Use Instagram's music library. It gets better distribution than imported audio.
5Trending audio = free reach. Check the Reels tab for songs with an arrow icon.
6No watermarks ever. Instagram deprioritizes content with TikTok or other watermarks.
7Hashtags in first comment, not in the caption. Keeps captions clean, hashtags still work.
8Mix in carousel posts. The algorithm favors accounts that use multiple content formats.

    30-Day Launch Plan

    Week-by-week goals to get Sip & Slay off the ground

    Week 1: Find Your Rhythm

    Goal: 5 reels posted
    • Set up Instagram creator account
    • Post your first reel from trending shorts
    • Establish your character overlay style
    • Post at least 5 reels by end of week
    • Focus on getting comfortable, not perfection

    Week 2: Improve Hooks

    Goal: 7 reels posted
    • Study which of your reels got the most views
    • Focus on the first 1-2 seconds (the hook)
    • Try your first podcast clip edit
    • Experiment with different reaction text styles
    • Start engaging with similar accounts daily

    Week 3: Double Down

    Goal: 7 reels posted
    • Analyze top-performing reels and replicate the formula
    • Mix shorts and podcast clips (2-3 podcast clips this week)
    • Try posting at different times, track what works
    • Add 1-2 carousel posts to the mix
    • Respond to every single comment

    Week 4: Refine and Scale

    Goal: 7-10 reels posted
    • You now have a content formula - optimize it
    • Batch-create: edit 2-3 reels in one sitting
    • Start building a backlog of edited clips
    • Track followers, views, and engagement weekly
    • Plan for month 2: collaborations, series concepts

    Day 30 Checkpoint

    By now you should have 25+ reels posted, a consistent style, growing engagement, and a feel for what your audience likes. Review your analytics and double down on what works.
